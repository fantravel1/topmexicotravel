Mexico Travel Budget Guide 2025

Daily Costs, Money-Saving Tips & Budget Breakdowns

Last updated: January 2025

Mexico offers incredible value for travelers—you can eat like a king on street food for $5 or splurge at world-class restaurants for a fraction of US/European prices. Whether you're backpacking on a shoestring or planning a luxury vacation, this guide breaks down exactly what to expect.

Quick Budget Overview

Couples traveling together can often reduce per-person costs by 20-30% on accommodations.}

Accommodation Costs

{<strong>Hostels:</strong> $10-25 USD/night (dorm bed)</p><ul><li>Private rooms in hostels: $30-60 USD/night</p><ul><li>Best hostel networks: Selina, Mayan Monkey, Che Hostels</p><p><strong>Budget Hotels:</strong> $30-60 USD/night</p><ul><li>Basic but clean, often family-run</p><ul><li>Expect AC, WiFi, private bathroom</p><p><strong>Mid-Range Hotels:</strong> $80-150 USD/night</p><ul><li>Boutique hotels, good locations</p><ul><li>Pools, restaurants, design-forward</p><p><strong>Luxury Hotels:</strong> $200-500+ USD/night</p><ul><li>5-star resorts, historic properties</p><ul><li>All-inclusive resorts: $300-600+ USD/night (includes food/drinks)</p><p><strong>Airbnb:</strong> $40-150 USD/night depending on location and size</p><ul><li>Often best value for groups or longer stays}

Food & Drink Costs

{<strong>Street Food:</strong></p><ul><li>Tacos: $0.50-1.50 USD each (order 3-5 for a meal)</p><ul><li>Tortas/sandwiches: $2-4 USD</p><ul><li>Tamales: $1-2 USD</p><ul><li>Fresh juice: $1-2 USD</p><ul><li>Budget meal: $3-5 USD</p><p><strong>Casual Restaurants:</strong></p><ul><li>Lunch special (comida corrida): $4-7 USD (soup, main, drink, dessert!)</p><ul><li>Dinner at casual restaurant: $8-15 USD per person</p><p><strong>Mid-Range Restaurants:</strong></p><ul><li>Main course: $12-25 USD</p><ul><li>Dinner with drinks: $30-50 USD per person</p><p><strong>Fine Dining:</strong></p><ul><li>Tasting menu at top restaurants: $80-150 USD per person</p><ul><li>Still 30-50% less than comparable restaurants in US/Europe</p><p><strong>Drinks:</strong></p><ul><li>Local beer (store): $1-2 USD</p><ul><li>Beer at bar/restaurant: $2-4 USD</p><ul><li>Craft cocktail: $6-12 USD</p><ul><li>Mezcal/tequila shot: $4-15 USD depending on quality}

Transportation Costs

{<strong>Getting Around Cities:</strong></p><ul><li>Metro (Mexico City): $0.30 USD per ride</p><ul><li>Uber/DiDi: $3-10 USD for typical rides</p><ul><li>Taxi (official stands): $5-15 USD for typical trips</p><ul><li>Colectivos (shared vans): $0.50-2 USD</p><p><strong>Between Cities:</strong></p><ul><li>ADO/First-class bus: $25-70 USD (Cancún-Mexico City ~$50)</p><ul><li>Second-class bus: $15-40 USD</p><ul><li>Flights: $50-150 USD (book on Volaris, Viva Aerobus for deals)</p><p><strong>Car Rental:</strong></p><ul><li>Economy car: $25-45 USD/day (plus insurance)</p><ul><li>Full insurance: Add $15-25 USD/day</p><ul><li>Gas: Similar to US prices ($1/liter, roughly $3.80/gallon)</p><ul><li>Tolls: $5-30 USD depending on route</p><p><strong>Airport Transfers:</strong></p><ul><li>Official taxi: $40-80 USD to main tourist areas</p><ul><li>Shuttle/ADO bus: $10-25 USD</p><ul><li>Uber (where available): $20-50 USD}

Money-Saving Tips

{1. <strong>Eat where locals eat:</strong> Follow the crowds to busy taquerias and market stalls. Best food, lowest prices.</p><p>2. <strong>Take advantage of comida corrida:</strong> The midday meal deal (1-4pm) at casual restaurants offers incredible value—soup, main, drink, and dessert for $4-7.</p><p>3. <strong>Use Uber/DiDi:</strong> Cheaper than taxis, no negotiating, and safer. Download both apps before arrival.</p><p>4. <strong>Travel overnight:</strong> Long-distance ADO buses have fully reclining seats. Take night buses to save on accommodation.</p><p>5. <strong>Stay longer:</strong> Weekly and monthly rentals on Airbnb can cut accommodation costs 30-50%.</p><p>6. <strong>Drink local:</strong> Mexican beer and mezcal are cheap. Imported wine and spirits are expensive.</p><p>7. <strong>Visit museums on Sunday:</strong> Many are free for Mexican residents, and some are free for everyone.</p><p>8. <strong>Book activities locally:</strong> Tours booked through hotels cost 20-50% more than booking direct at the site or with local operators.</p><p>9. <strong>Carry cash:</strong> Many small businesses, markets, and street food don't take cards. ATMs are everywhere.</p><p>10. <strong>Skip tourist restaurants:</strong> Restaurants on main plazas charge 30-50% more. Walk one block away for better food at lower prices.}

Money & Payments

{<strong>Currency:</strong> Mexican Peso (MXN). As of 2025, approximately 17-18 pesos = 1 USD.</p><p><strong>Cash vs. Cards:</strong></p><ul><li>Cards widely accepted at hotels, restaurants, shops</p><ul><li>Cash essential for markets, street food, small businesses, tips</p><ul><li>Always carry some cash—ATM lines can be long in tourist areas</p><p><strong>ATMs:</strong></p><ul><li>Use ATMs inside banks for security</p><ul><li>Decline the ATM's exchange rate—your bank's rate is always better</p><ul><li>Withdraw larger amounts less frequently to minimize fees</p><ul><li>Notify your bank of travel plans to avoid blocks</p><p><strong>Tipping:</strong></p><ul><li>Restaurants: 15-20% (check if service charge included)</p><ul><li>Bars: 10-15%</p><ul><li>Hotel staff: $2-5 USD/day for housekeeping</p><ul><li>Tour guides: $5-10 USD per person</p><ul><li>Taxi/Uber: Not expected but round up appreciated}

Frequently Asked Questions

How much money should I bring to Mexico for a week?

For a comfortable mid-range trip, budget $600-1,000 USD per person for a week (excluding flights). This covers good hotels, restaurant meals, activities, and transport. Budget travelers can manage on $250-350, while luxury travelers should plan $1,500-2,500+.

Is it cheaper to pay in pesos or dollars?

Always pay in pesos. While some tourist businesses accept dollars, they use unfavorable exchange rates. Withdraw pesos from ATMs (declining the machine's conversion rate) or use cards that don't charge foreign transaction fees.

How much should I tip in Mexico?

Tip 15-20% at restaurants (check if service is included), $2-5 USD per day for hotel housekeeping, $5-10 per person for tour guides, and round up taxi fares. Tips are genuinely appreciated as wages are low.

Are all-inclusive resorts worth it?

It depends. For pure beach relaxation with unlimited food and drink, all-inclusives can be good value ($200-400/day per person). However, you'll miss Mexico's incredible independent restaurants and cultural experiences. Consider splitting your trip—all-inclusive for relaxation, then independent travel for exploration.
