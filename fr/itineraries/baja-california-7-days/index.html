7 Days in Baja California Sur

Desert Landscapes, Whale Watching & Beach Paradise

Baja California Sur is Mexico's final frontier—a dramatic peninsula where the desert plunges into two seas, whales come to give birth, and artistic communities thrive in oasis towns. This 7-day itinerary combines the resort energy of Los Cabos with the bohemian soul of Todos Santos and the natural wonders of La Paz, creating the perfect Baja experience.

Trip Highlights

  • Watch whales at close range in Magdalena Bay
  • Swim with whale sharks in La Paz
  • Explore the Hotel California legend
  • Kayak with sea lions at Espíritu Santo
  • See El Arco at Land's End
  • Taste farm-to-table cuisine in Todos Santos
Duration
7 Days
Best Time
December to April for whale watching and perfect weather
Budget (Mid-Range)
$1,500-2,500 USD per person

Day-by-Day Itinerary

1

Arrive in Los Cabos

San José del Cabo

Fly into Los Cabos International Airport (SJD) and transfer to San José del Cabo, the quieter, more charming of the two Cabos. Check into your hotel and explore the historic art district with its colonial church, galleries, and Thursday Art Walk (if timing allows). Dine at one of the excellent restaurants on the plaza.

Practical Information

Getting There

Fly into Los Cabos International Airport (SJD). Major airlines serve SJD from most US cities and many international destinations. La Paz has a small airport (LAP) with limited connections.

Getting Around

Rent a car—essential for this itinerary. Roads are excellent and scenic. The toll-free highway between Cabos and La Paz is safe and beautiful. Some tour operators provide transport from hotels.

Frequently Asked Questions

When is whale watching season in Baja?

Gray whales visit Baja's Pacific lagoons (Magdalena Bay, San Ignacio) from December to April, with February being peak season. Whale sharks are in the Sea of Cortez near La Paz from October to April. Humpback whales pass through Los Cabos November to March.

Is it safe to swim with whale sharks?

Yes, absolutely. Whale sharks are gentle filter feeders—they eat plankton, not people. They can grow up to 40 feet but are completely docile. Reputable operators follow strict guidelines for safe, respectful encounters.

Can I do this trip without a car?

It's possible but limiting. You could base in Los Cabos and take day tours to Todos Santos and La Paz. However, a rental car gives you flexibility to explore at your own pace and stop at scenic viewpoints along the way.

Is Los Cabos expensive?

The Cabo corridor has resort pricing—expect to pay US-level prices at hotels and high-end restaurants. However, Todos Santos and La Paz are more affordable, and tacos are cheap everywhere. A mid-range trip is very doable.
